its "own" CSV is a *filtered extract of that same POS*.

## 3. Wages — rebuilt from Xero actuals

**The rule:** a salaried employee costs `annual / 52` per week. Every week.
Regardless of hours logged in Deputy. Verified against Xero payslips — every
salaried person is paid exactly 40 units/week at their contract rate, and leave
sits *inside* the 40 (Kris: 38.5 worked + 1.5 leave = 40).

The old model used `hours_logged × (annual/52/40)`, assuming hours logged == hours
paid. Kris logged 16h of a 40h week → $719 instead of $1,798.

**Now:**
- **Closed weeks** — costed from `data/xero_pay_weekly.json` (what payroll actually
  paid), allocated pro-rata across logged shifts. Hours decide WHERE the cost
  lands; Xero decides how much.
- **The open week** — estimated via `scripts/wage_model.py`.
- `rebuild_wages.py` runs nightly (7:15am) over the current + previous payroll
  week. **Load-bearing, not belt-and-braces:** salaried cost is only knowable once
  a week is known, AND Deputy's Cost lands on *approval* (25 Jun's shifts were
  approved 30 Jun and never re-read). Re-reading the fortnight fixes both.

**History rebuilt:** 90 weeks, 603 days. 2,443 employee-weeks from Xero actuals,
943 estimated. Recovered **$268k** of previously invisible labour — including 13
departed staff (Udaya Moktan 1,184h, Vincent 1,073h, Ferry Anggriawan 992h…)
whose cost was missing entirely because Xero's Employees endpoint returns
**ACTIVE only** (45) while 100 pay runs name **122** people.

**Impact:** FY-to-date group wage 37.4% → **41.1%**. HG 51.9% → **58.0%**.

**Refresh the Xero side on the Mac** (token rotates; Actions would burn it):
```
python3 scripts/pull_xero_pay_weekly.py     # -> data/xero_pay_weekly.json
# then dispatch: Employee Map, then Rebuild Wages
```

**DEPRECATED — do not run:** `backfill_wages_deputy.py`, `backfill_dept_split.py`.
Both exit immediately. They cost salaried staff at hours × rate and would write
the old model back over the rebuild.

**New salary-earners** are caught weekly by `check_salaried_roster.py` (launchd
`com.stowaway.salariedcheck`, Mondays 10:40, on the Mac). Pujan Tamang ($75k,

## 4. Xero audit — every account, every line

Audited all P&L accounts Apr–Jun against every rule:

- **`Super Guarantee Interest & Admin Fee`** ($12.6k/qtr, ~$50k/yr) was counted
  **nowhere** — lacks "superannuation" so missed payroll, contains "interest" so
  overheads dropped it. **Fixed** into `group_payroll`. Note what it *is*: the ATO
  charging for **super paid late**. April +$8,538, June +$4,084 — lumpy,
  consistent with a catch-up charge.
- **`To Be Reviewed`** tracking option wasn't in `MISC_OPTIONS` (which only
  matched names starting "Miscellaneous"). Held $14,242.91 of Rent - Stowaway.
  **Now excluded.** Unknown options are now **reported with their dollar value**.
- **Interest + depreciation** (~$69k/yr) were excluded from every feed. Now emitted
  as `group_finance` and surfaced admin-side as an **`Interest & dep`** segment,
  last before Profit on the "where each revenue dollar went" strip — so the green
  tail is **net**, not operating. **Deliberately NOT in overheads**: overheads drive
  breakeven, and you can't roster your way out of a loan. `breakevenParts` untouched.
- **COGS maps cleanly**: 97.2% venue-allocated, 2.2% Misc (correctly excluded),
  0.6% ($1,263) tagged in neither category.
- **Rent** left on Olly's BEP plan basis per Zak. For the record: HG matches Xero
